Forexionary - Forex Dictionary

Term: Currency Swap
Definition: A "currency swap" is a financial agreement between two parties to exchange principal and interest in one currency for the same in another currency. It is often used for risk management or to access better interest rates.
Term: Slippage
Definition: "Slippage" is the difference between the expected price of a trade and the price at which it is executed. It can occur during fast-moving markets or due to broker delays.
Term: Drawdown
Definition: "Drawdown" represents the peak-to-trough decline during a specific trading period. It measures the percentage loss in a trading account from its highest point.
Term: Scalping
Definition: "Scalping" is a short-term trading strategy where traders aim to make small profits by rapidly entering and exiting trades, often within seconds or minutes.
Term: Fibonacci Retracement
Definition: "Fibonacci retracement" is a technical analysis tool that helps identify potential support and resistance levels based on key Fibonacci ratios, often used to predict price reversals.
Term: Risk-Reward Ratio
Definition: The "risk-reward ratio" is a measure of potential profit relative to potential loss in a trade. It is used to assess the risk and reward of a trading strategy.
Term: Carry Currency
Definition: A "carry currency" is a currency with a high-interest rate that is often used in a carry trade, where traders borrow in low-interest currencies to invest in high-interest currencies.
Term: Central Bank Intervention
Definition: "Central bank intervention" refers to actions taken by a country's central bank to influence its currency's exchange rate, such as buying or selling its own currency in the foreign exchange market.
Term: Technical Indicator
Definition: A "technical indicator" is a mathematical calculation used to forecast price movements or market conditions in forex trading, such as moving averages, RSI, or MACD.
Term: Cross Currency Pair
Definition: A "cross currency pair" is a currency pair that does not include the U.S. dollar. It involves trading two major currencies without the need for the USD.
Term: Order Book
Definition: An "order book" is a real-time list of buy and sell orders in the market for a specific currency pair, providing insight into supply and demand.
Term: Forex Signal
Definition: A "forex signal" is a recommendation to enter or exit a trade, typically generated by a professional trader or trading software. It provides guidance to other traders.
Term: Moving Average
Definition: A "moving average" is a statistical calculation used to analyze and smooth out price data over a specific period. It helps identify trends and potential entry or exit points.
Term: Carry Trade
Definition: A "carry trade" is a trading strategy where an investor borrows money in a currency with a low-interest rate and invests it in a currency with a higher interest rate to profit from the interest rate differential.
Term: Hedging
Definition: "Hedging" is a risk management strategy that involves opening a position to offset potential losses in another position. It aims to reduce overall risk.
Term: Carry Trade
Definition: A "carry trade" is a trading strategy where an investor borrows money in a currency with a low-interest rate and invests it in a currency with a higher interest rate to profit from the interest rate differential.
Term: Margin Call
Definition: A "margin call" is a request from a broker to deposit additional funds into an account when losses exceed available margin. Failure to meet a margin call may result in positions being closed.
Term: Liquidity Provider
Definition: A "liquidity provider" is a financial institution that offers buy and sell quotes for financial instruments, helping ensure market liquidity and efficient trade execution.
Term: Candlestick Chart
Definition: A "candlestick chart" is a type of chart used in technical analysis that displays price movements and patterns. Each "candle" represents a specific time frame and provides information on open, high, low, and close prices.
Term: Fundamental Analysis
Definition: "Fundamental analysis" is an approach to analyzing the forex market that focuses on economic and political factors, such as economic indicators, interest rates, and government policies.
Term: Leverage
Definition: "Leverage" allows traders to control a larger position with a smaller amount of capital. While it can amplify profits, it also increases the potential for losses and should be used with caution.
